The "Human Verification" Loop

A site that asks you to fill out three surveys, download a mobile game, or "verify you are human" by clicking on links that look fishy is a scam. These sites don't have access to "cheating buster ai" technology; they make money off of your clicks and don't give you any results.

This is the biggest warning sign of all. A real "cheaterbuster ai alternative free" shouldn't ask for your credit card "just to verify your age" or "to keep the service free." Once they have your numbers, you'll often find unauthorized monthly subscriptions for "premium support" that are almost impossible to cancel.

"Sign in with Google/Facebook" Prompts

Be very careful of any unverified cheating buster AI site that asks you to log in with your own social media account. This is a common "phishing" trick that hackers use to take over your accounts. Always use the manual methods, "Google Dorking" or "Social Searcher" instead, because they don't require you to give up your own digital keys.
